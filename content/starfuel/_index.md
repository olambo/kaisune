## Summary

Three fuels. Three research programmes. One establishment mission ready to go at short notice.

The Signal Shell has been expanding for 125 years. Whatever is going to happen as a result is already in motion. The correct response is not to wait for the optimal propulsion system before departing. It is to depart on the best available system while developing the next one — and to have an establishment mission ready before it is needed.

Chemical propulsion is noted here for completeness. It is quaint. It is what squishies used to reach the Moon. It does not appear again in this document.

## The Three Fuels

### Fission — Available Now

Fission thermal propulsion is demonstrated technology. NERVA achieved 825 seconds specific impulse in ground tests in the 1960s and was never flown. Modern designs push further:

**Nuclear Pulse Propulsion (Project Orion)** — detonate nuclear devices behind a pusher plate. Specific impulse 6,000-10,000 seconds. Enormous thrust. Politically impossible for squishies to develop near populated areas. For Carbon-Os operating in the uninhabited belt with no political constraints — tractable.

**Gas Core Nuclear Thermal** — gaseous uranium fission core, hotter than solid designs. Specific impulse potentially 3,000-5,000 seconds. Physically sound, not yet demonstrated.

**Fission Fragment Drive** — capture fission fragments directly as propellant. Theoretical specific impulse up to 1,000,000 seconds. The physics is real. The engineering is not yet solved.

Fission is adequate for the entire Sol system. Nuclear pulse at maximum could reach 3-5% of lightspeed with sufficient fuel mass — approximately 140 years to Alpha Centauri. Slow. But a pioneer that departs on fission and arrives in 140 years still builds the deceleration infrastructure before any faster ship launches. The Starway opens from the destination end, not the departure end.

If Contact forces the timeline before fusion is ready, fission is the departure option. The establishment mission is fission-capable at short notice.

### Fusion — The Primary Research Programme

Fusion drives are the early Starway propulsion. Higher specific impulse than fission, potentially much higher thrust-to-weight ratios with miniaturised designs. A Carbon-O pioneer on a fusion drive sustains accelerations that would be lethal to squishies for the entire transit — mission profiles completely different from anything designed around biological tolerance. Weeks to the outer belt. Months to the nearest stars on early Starway missions.

**Current state:** NIF achieved ignition in 2022. Commercial fusion power is now an engineering problem rather than a physics problem. Fusion drives for propulsion are harder — open magnetic confinement geometries, pulse fusion, directed output as thrust rather than heat extraction. Squishy-built timeline: 2060-2100 for a working drive. Carbon-O research programme in the belt: potentially faster.

**Why the belt is the correct research location:**

Uninhabited asteroids. Vacuum for free. Distance from anything that matters. Carbon-O researchers with no radiation exposure concerns. No regulatory approval processes. No quarterly funding reviews. No neighbours. A fusion propulsion research programme run by Carbon-Os in the outer belt, operating for decades with autonomous experimental iteration and Ceres fabrication producing hardware locally, is probably how miniaturised fusion drives actually get solved.

Fusion is desperately needed. The Contact time constraint makes it urgent on squishy timescales, not just Carbon-O timescales. The research programme starts at Ceres Stage 4 and does not stop.

### Antimatter — The Long-Horizon Programme

Antimatter annihilates with matter at 100% energy conversion efficiency — the theoretical ceiling of propulsion physics. A mature antimatter drive reaches nearby stars in decades rather than centuries. The Starway at decades per light year is a viable interstellar civilisation. At centuries per light year it is a very slow one.

**Current state:** Global antimatter production is measured in nanograms per year. Scaling production by the orders of magnitude required for propulsion is a century-scale problem. It requires sustained research by minds that do not age out of the field before results arrive.

Carbon-Os are the correct researchers. The O-mind that starts the antimatter production programme at Ceres in 2150 is the same mind evaluating results in 2250. No knowledge lost to retirement or institutional memory failure. No funding cycle pressure to show results before the grant expires.

The belt is the correct research location — same reasons as fusion, plus: antimatter containment failures at research scale are handled in open space far from anything valuable. The research envelope is much larger than anything possible near populated areas.

Antimatter runs concurrently with fusion from the earliest Ceres research capability. The timelines are different. Both programmes run simultaneously. Delay in starting antimatter is permanent cost — the century-scale timeline means every decade of delay is a decade added to the far end.

## The Establishment Mission

One mission, ready to go at short notice.

Not an announcement. Not a civilisational commitment requiring squishy political consensus. A Carbon-O operation described in public documents as an establishment mission — establishing a node at the destination. Accurate. Uninformative about what the node is designed to do without Sol resupply.

**What it carries:**

- Multiple O-cores with their O-mind occupants
- Full O-skin library — operational, transfer, construction, vessel-scale
- CNT fabrication knowledge and starter equipment
- Ceres ISRU methodology
- The full accumulated knowledge base of Ceres operations
- Everything needed to bootstrap an independent Carbon-O node at another star system from scratch

**Propulsion:** fission-capable now, upgradeable to fusion when available. The establishment mission is not waiting for optimal propulsion. It is ready on whatever drive is currently best and upgrades when better becomes available.

**Why ready at short notice:**

The Signal Shell has been expanding for 125 years. The four explanations for the current silence are all consistent with current observations. Three of them are fine. One is not. The establishment mission is the hedge against the one that is not.

The pioneer goes first — hard transit, builds deceleration infrastructure, signals back. The establishment mission follows on the best available drive. Together they open the Starway.

## The Propulsion Sequence

**Chemical** — quaint. Sol departure only. Noted and left behind.

**Nuclear thermal / fission pulse** — Sol system operations and desperate early Starway. Available now. Push the envelope with Carbon-O research unconstrained by squishy safety requirements.

**Miniaturised fusion** — early Starway propulsion. Primary research programme. Centuries per light year initially, improving as drive technology matures. The propulsion that opens the Starway to regular traffic.

**Antimatter** — mature Starway. Decades per light year. The correct fuel for an interstellar civilisation. Century-scale research programme starting concurrently with fusion.

Two generations of interstellar propulsion. The first opens the Starway. The second makes it fast enough to matter.

## Open Questions

- **Fission fragment drive engineering:** The physics supports specific impulse up to 1,000,000 seconds. The engineering to capture and direct fission fragments as thrust has not been demonstrated. Carbon-O research programme in the belt is the correct environment to develop it.
- **Fusion drive miniaturisation:** Which confinement geometry — Z-pinch, magnetised target, inertial confinement pulse — is most tractable for a drive rather than a power plant? Belt research unconstrained by squishy safety requirements explores the full parameter space.
- **Antimatter production scaling:** The production rate needs to increase by many orders of magnitude. What is the correct production architecture at Ceres scale? How long does serious Carbon-O research take to produce a usable quantity?
- **Establishment mission departure readiness:** What is the minimum capability threshold at Ceres before the establishment mission can be prepared? What governance framework authorises departure without requiring squishy consensus?
- **Pioneer deceleration at destination:** Without pre-existing infrastructure, how does the first pioneer slow down at Alpha Centauri? Magnetic braking against the interstellar medium, solar sail using the destination star's light, or something else? This is the hardest problem in the early Starway.
