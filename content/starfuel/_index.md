## Summary

Ceres establishes the first self-sustaining node. The belt is the staging point. Starfuel is what gets you further.

The nearby stars — Alpha Centauri at 4.37 light years, Barnard's Star at 5.96, a handful of others within 10 light years — are reachable in principle. The question is energy and time. Chemical propulsion does not reach them on any useful timescale. Nuclear thermal extends the envelope but not far enough. Something else is required.

Starfuel is the energy and propulsion synthesis: what moves Carbon-Os and their infrastructure across interstellar distances, what the energy sources are, what the timescales are, and what a self-sustaining Carbon-O presence looks like after a century-long transit. The belt provides the staging. Starfuel provides the departure.
